Homeroom

A 4 player game in which students help each other escape the police.

Timeline

One Month

Nov-Dec 2020

My Role

Level Designer, Developer

Tools

Node.js, Socket.io, Unity, Autodesk Maya

For Homeroom, I collaborated with 3 other artists to create a short game. Homeroom is a collaborative game for 4 players in which one player is targeted by police. The other 3 players need to utilize the classroom and each other to help block the police from reaching their target and help their classmate escape! Everything in Homeroom from the music to the animations to the server code was made from scratch.

Early Designs

We wanted Homeroom to have a unique and fun style. Inspired by games such as Fall Guys and Starve Together we went for a flat, colorful and cartoonish style, making the heads and eyes on our characters very large and the models fairly low-poly.

Game Mechanics

Homeroom is fundamentally a team based game based on cooperation and we wanted our mechanics to reflect that. The mechanics we created are meant to inspire quick thinking and team tactics, with the game being hard to play without proper communication.

Movement

We designed our levels to be based on a grid, this allows our players to clearly see the more rigid, robotic movement of the police agents and predict where they would go. The targeted player is slower than the agents, making them an easy catch if they go without help from their classmates.

Body Blocking

The main way for players to slow the police agents is to slow them with their own body. This allows players to form barricades, kiting and slowing agents away from the door for an escape.

Desks

Desks function as a way to inhibit the movement of the police agents and the players. We gave the non-targeted players the ability to slowly push desks and manipulate the level. With proper timing this allows players to set traps and reroute police agents, turning each level into a kind of puzzle and allowing emergent opportunities to arise for creative players.

Backpacks

Backpacks can be picked up and spilled onto the ground to further slow a police agent down. We added this mechanic as a way to balance the game, making levels harding in layout but adding backpacks to even it out.

Testing

We used a quick mockup of our game made in p5js and networked with node.js and socket.io to quickly test out mechanics and enemy AI. This allowed us to see how “fun” our game was with a variety of players and make quick adjustments before really refining its visual style.

Level Design

Due to the limited, grid-like nature of our levels, proper level design was key to making the game balanced and fun. We started with various simple mockups made in photoshop and played through them on paper with one tester acting as the AI and 4 testers acting as people.

Our goal with each level was to slowly introduce each mechanic into the game and require more precise teamwork. For example, the first level arranges the desks in such a way that none of them can be pushed, thus forcing players to learn how to use their bodies only to block the police agent.
