Tartan Trucks

Tartan Trucks was a project created for the class Interaction Design Overview at Carnegie Mellon. The goal was to create a mobile app concept for CMU's on campus food trucks.

Timeline

March 2019

My Role

UX Designer

Tools

Adobe Indesign, Adobe XD

Research

In order to make informed design decisions on this app, we started by identifying the kinds of people that will be using this app and what kind of experience they would want from it. We then interviewed students, staff, and the food truck owners themselves and made adjustments to our design.

While hypothetical personas are helpful, real conclusions can only be drawn from actual interviews. We prepared some basic questions about general app functionality and features and took to interviewing students and food truck owners.

Developement

The next step was to think of the pages that this app would have and create a basic task flow with post-its.

From there it was a matter of creating wireframes and tweaking until we were happy.

User Testing

Once we had a nice interactive wireframe version of the app we had a few potential users come in and test the app for us. We had them do various tasks ranging from ordering something new after browsing the menu to leaving reviews. We gave them tips such as think outloud etc.

User testing allowed us to streamline our design a lot and consider design choices we never would have alone. It also identified bugs or missing features in our design such as bad app navigation or non-intuitive layouts.
