FASARA™ + DI-NOC™

AR as a tool to easily showcase extensive product catalogues.

As a Salesperson at 3M...

... A sale is less about finding one product to suit the client's needs and more about narrowing down the already extensive list of 3M products into a curated selection. Within the realm of architectural finishes specifically it is up to the salesperson to walk the client through the in's and out's of 3M's film catalogue, helping the client grasp which materials and patterns would best suit a space.

The challenge quickly becomes how best to showcase so many material samples quickly and effectively. Mockup images can only go so far, samples dont capture a sense of scale, and setting up display of the product takes time and effort.

A lightweight, mobile AR solution would allow 3M to quickly showcase product catalogues at any scale, in any context.

Recreating 3M Archtictural Finishes

Before beginning the whole design process for this tool it was first crutial to create digital samples of the products to see it they can hold up to the real thing. After all, what's the point of a product showcase demo that can't properly showcase the products?

Working with Unity's URP shader library and some custom GLSL, I first began recreating FASARA™ patterns and blurs. FASARA™ Glass Finishes are semi-translucent, textured films that, when applied to glass, can be used to control the amout of light and privacy in a space while enhancing the interior's aesthetics.

Later on the app's usage branched out to include the DI-NOC™ product line as well. DI-NOC™ is a thick, printed film that mimics the roughness, specularity, and color of other materials for a fraction of the cost.

After a lot of tweaking, we deemed the AR materials a success. Next I worked with 3M Japan's design team to review sales points of the products that the app should drive home such as flexable pattern size and color as well as possible custom material tweaks.

Simplicty & Speed

After talks with salespeople and news of a potential use in upcoming exhibitions we decided that the app needed to have a focus on ease-of-use as clients would likely only use it once or twice and for very small windows of time. This meant streamlining the app greatly and cutting many planned features.

First Prototype

Below is a walk-through of the finished mobile prototype I created in unity. This inital version was showcased briefly at 3M's booth in Milan Design Week 2022 as well as used breifly in product sales pitches company wide.

Improvements and Pivots

After success in Milan, the apps usage expanded to include a showcase of DI-NOC product line as well. Learning from the app's drawbacks when initially showcased, we focused more on planning the both around the app to work in tandem with the app itself. Lighting was pre-planned to make materials fit the space seamlessly and a QR was used to quickly and accurately place samples and panels of information.
